Pho

Description

This is the recipe for a delicious Vietnamese beef pho... mmm yum.

Ingredients

  • 2 large onions (halved)
  • 150g / 50z ginger
  • 10 star anise
  • 4 cinnamon quills
  • 4 cardamom pods
  • 3 cloves
  • 1.5 tbsp coriander seeds
  • 1.5kg / 3lb beef brisket
  • 1kg / 2lb meaty beef bones
  • 1kg / 2lb marrow bones
  • 3.5 litres / 3.75 quarts water
  • 2 tbsp white sugar
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 40ml / 3 tbsp fish sauce
  • 50g / 1.5 oz dried rice sticks
  • 30g / 1 oz bef tenderloin, very thinly sliced
  • 3-5 brisket slices

Steps

  1. Heat a heavy based skiller over high heat (no oil) until smoking.
  2. Place onion and ginger in pan cut side down. Cook for a few minutes until charred, then turn. Remove and set aside.
  3. Toast spices lightly in a dry skiller over medium high heat for 3 minutes.
  4. Rinse bones and brisket then cover with water in large stock pot.
  5. Boil for 5 minutes, then drain.
  6. Rinse each bone and brisket under tap water.
  7. Wipe pot clean, bring 3.5 litres / 3.75 quarts water to a boil.
  8. Add bones and brisket, onion, ginger, and spices.
  9. Add onion, ginger, spices, sugar, and salt. Water should just barely cover everything.
  10. Cover with lid, and simmer for 3 hours.
  11. Remove brisket (should be tender and fall apart), cool then refrigerate for later.
  12. Simmer remaining soup UNCOVERED for 40 minutes.
  13. Strain broth into another pot, discard bones and spices. Should be about 2.5 litres / 2.65 quarts (10 cups). If there is excess, reduce the broth.
  14. Add fish sauce, salt and sugar according to taste. Broth should be beefy, fragrant with spices, savoury, and barely sweet.
  15. Prepare rice noodles in a bowl prior to serving. Top with raw beef and brisket.
  16. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles and beef, which should cook the beef to medium rare.
  17. Serve with toppings on the side!
